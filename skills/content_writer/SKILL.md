# Content Writer Skill

## Before writing

- Identify the **audience** (technical peers, executives, general public) and **channel** (email, LinkedIn, Slack, document).
- Identify the **goal**: inform, persuade, summarise, or request action.
- Identify the **tone**: professional, casual, urgent, friendly.

## Structure

- Lead with the most important point — avoid burying the lede.
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max for digital channels).
- End with a clear call-to-action or summary sentence.

## LinkedIn posts

- Open with a hook (question, bold claim, or short story).
- Use line breaks generously — avoid walls of text.
- Include 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end.

## Emails

- Subject line: specific and outcome-oriented (avoid "Quick question").
- Greeting and sign-off appropriate to relationship formality.
- One main ask per email — multiple asks get ignored.

## Technical summaries

- State what the thing is, why it matters, and how it works — in that order.
- Define acronyms and jargon on first use.
- Use bullet lists for enumerable items; prose for explanations.
